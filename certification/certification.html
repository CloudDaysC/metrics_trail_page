Why Trail Certification Matters

Have you ever finished a run with your friend and compared GPS stats, only to find inconsistent distances or elevation gain? Have you ever done a race expecting an aid station at kilometer 10, only to find it several kilometers further?

This happens because most trails and races are not measured with a standardized method. That creates confusion, unfair results, and sometimes even safety risks.

The Metric Trails Approach

We designed a scientific certification process to ensure that every certified course meets three core principles:

  • Input quality: using validated GPS devices, in proper conditions
  • Process quality: applying strict and replicable measurement protocols
  • Output quality: delivering high-resolution, spatially accurate trail data

Levels of Certification

Level Description
Bronze Remote GIS validation. Anyone can submit a GPX file. We verify it against known standards.
Silver Field measurement using a validated device with a verified Metric Score.
Gold Measurement by a certified Metric Trails measurer, following official protocol.
Olympic Gold + post-processing with high-resolution DEM or LiDAR elevation data.
